# PART 1 — THE BEGINNING

---

# 1. Why AskSky Exists

I wanted to build more than just another tutorial project.

Most weather applications created by students demonstrate only technical implementation. They rarely explain product thinking, architecture, documentation, scalability, security, or long-term engineering decisions.

I wanted one project that could teach me the complete software development lifecycle—from product vision to deployment.

AskSky became my classroom, my playground, and my flagship project.

---

# 2. The Problem

Weather influences millions of decisions every day.

- Travel
- Agriculture
- Health
- Education
- Outdoor activities
- Aviation
- Business operations

Existing weather applications provide large amounts of data but often fail to explain what the weather actually means for the user.

Weather information should help people make better decisions—not just display numbers.

---

# 3. Why Another Weather App?

AskSky is not another clone of an existing weather application.

It is designed to explore how a modern software product should be planned, designed, documented, engineered, and maintained.

AskSky focuses on:

- AI-powered weather insights
- Clean and minimal user experience
- Enterprise-grade architecture
- Scalable backend design
- Developer-first engineering practices
- Long-term maintainability

The goal is to learn product engineering—not simply build features.

---

# 4. The Vision

The long-term vision is to build an intelligent weather platform that helps people make better daily decisions.

AskSky aims to combine:

- Weather data
- Artificial Intelligence
- Beautiful design
- Excellent user experience
- Modern engineering
- Reliable architecture

Technology should make weather easier to understand.

---

# 5. The Founder

AskSky is created and led by **Gayathri Palraj**.

I am a Computer Science and Engineering graduate from Chennai, India.

I believe great software begins with understanding the problem before writing code.

AskSky represents my commitment to becoming not only a programmer, but a professional software engineer.
